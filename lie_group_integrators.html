Lie group integrators¶

The formulation of differential equations on manifolds¶

Lie group integrators solve differential equations whose solution evolve on a manifold \(\mathcal{M}\). This means that we seek a curve \(y(t)\in\mathcal{M}\) whose tangent at any point coincides with a vector field \(F\in\mathcal{X}(\mathcal{M})\) and passing through a designated initial value \(y_0\) at \(t=t_0\)

(1)¶\[\begin{align} \dot{y}(t) = F|_{y(t)},\qquad y(t_0)=y_0. \end{align}\]

Before addressing numerical methods for solving (1) it is necessary to introduce a convenient way of representing the vector field \(F\). We furnish \(\mathcal{M}\) with a transitive action \(\Psi: G \times \mathcal{M} \rightarrow \mathcal{M}\) by some Lie group \(G\) of dimension \(d\geq\dim \mathcal{M}\). We denote the action of \(g\) on \(m\) as \(g\cdot m\), i.e. \(g\cdot m=\Psi(g,m)\). Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be the Lie algebra of \(G\), and denote by \(\exp: \mathfrak{g}\rightarrow G\) the exponential map. We define \(\Psi_*:\mathfrak{g}\rightarrow\mathcal{X}(\mathcal{M})\) to be the infinitesimal generator of the action, i.e.

(2)¶\[\begin{align} \left.F_\xi\right|_m= \left.\Psi_*(\xi)\right|_m = \left.\frac{d}{dt}\right|_{t=0} \Psi(\exp(t\xi), m) \end{align}\]

The transitivity of the action now ensures that \(\left.\Psi_*(\mathfrak{g})\right|_m=T_m\mathcal{M}\) for any \(m\in\mathcal{M}\), such that any tangent vector \(v_m\in T_m\mathcal{M}\) can be represented as \(v_m=\left.\Psi_*(\xi_v)\right|_m\) for some \(\xi_v\in\mathfrak{g}\) (\(\xi_v\) may not be unique). Consequently, for any vector field \(F\in\mathcal{X}(\mathcal{M})\) there exists a map \(f:\mathcal{M}\rightarrow\mathfrak{g}\) } such that

(3)¶\[\begin{align} F|_m = \left.\Psi_*(f(m))\right|_m,\quad\text{for all}\; m\in \mathcal{M} \end{align}\]

This is the original tool from (H. Munthe-Kaas, (1999) Appl. Numer. Math 29) for representing a vector field on a manifold with a group action.
